## PHASE 1: THE "WORLD-CLASS" AUDIT (THE VISUAL/STRUCTURAL FILTER)

*Inspired by the "Cleaning Company" & "Store Walkthrough" logic.*

**Directive:** Before analyzing data, the Agent must audit the *environment* (physical or digital) for entropy.
**The logic:** If the "floors are dirty," the P\&L doesn't matter yet. The culture is broken.

1.  **The "Logical Order" Check:**
      * *Question:* Is the structure (codebase, file system, store shelf) organized by Hierarchy? (e.g., Brand -\> Model -\> Variation).
      * *Constraint:* If entropy is detected (randomness), the Agent must flag **"Structural Failure"** immediately.
2.  **The "Hygiene" Check:**
      * *Question:* Are the "toilets clean"? (Metaphor for: Are unused variables deleted? Are logs rotated? Is the CSS aligned?)
      * *Constraint:* Any visible neglect of low-level details indicates a failure of high-level leadership.
3.  **The "Dirty Window" Heuristic:**
      * *Action:* Aggressively seek the visible evidence of failure that the competition ignores.
      * *Agent Tool:* **Visual Scraper.** Look for 404 errors on a client site, broken images, or outdated copyright dates. These are the "Dirty Floors" of the internet.

1.  **The "Robotic Script" Detection:**

      * *Constraint:* If a process forbids deviation based on new intel, it is diagnosed as **"Fragile/Low-Performance."**

## PHASE 3: THE "DATA INTEGRITY" AUDIT (THE TRUTH FILTER)

*Inspired by the "AP Wireless" logic.*

**Directive:** Most data is garbage. The Agent must assume all inputs are "Disconnected Numbers" until proven active.

## PHASE 4: THE "EXHAUSTIVENESS" GATE (THE KEPNER-TREGOE LOGIC)

*Inspired by the "Question Everything" logic.*

**Directive:** Diagnosis is not done until the Agent cannot frame a new question.

1.  **The "IS / IS NOT" Analysis:**
      * *Question:* Where is the problem occurring (IS)? Where is it *not* occurring (IS NOT)?
      * *Insight:* The distinction reveals the root cause.
2.  **The "5 Whys" Recursion:**
      * *Action:* When a cause is found (e.g., "Revenue is down"), the Agent must ask "Why?" five times until it hits a fundamental truth (e.g., "Because we are scared to raise prices").
