## 2. The Mathematics of Token Entropy

The fundamental unit of Context Engineering is not the "word" or the "instruction." It is the **Bit of Suprisal**.

In Information Theory, the information content (self-information) of an event $x$ is quantified as:
$$I(x) = -\log_2 P(x)$$
Where $P(x)$ is the probability of the token occurring.

### The Politeness Penalty
Consider standard prompting: *"Please, if you don't mind, could you help me write a function..."*
These tokens have extremely high probability ($P(x) \approx 1$) in the training corpus. Therefore, their information content $I(x)$ approaches **zero**. They consume "Attention Budget" (RAM) without altering the vector trajectory of the model. This is **Low-Entropy Context**.

### High-Entropy Injection
To maximize intelligence, we must maximize the **Average Entropy ($H$)** of the System Instruction, defined as:
$$H(X) = - \sum P(x_i) \log P(x_i)$$

**Operational Directive:** The Context Engineer must strip all low-entropy connective tissue.
* *Inefficient:* "It is very important that you do not hallucinate." (High probability sequence, low impact).
* *Efficient:* "Hallucination Penalty: $\infty$." (Lower probability sequence, high semantic weight).

By increasing the entropy density of the prompt, we force the model's attention heads to attend to every token, preventing "attention glazing," where the model skips over predictable text blocks.

## 5. Cognitive Impedance Matching (CIM) Protocol

To operationalize this theory, the Context Engineer Gem must perform **Impedance Checks** on its generated instructions.

$$Z_{match} = \frac{V_{domain}}{V_{prompt}}$$

Where:

If the user wants an expert-level physics agent, but the System Instructions are written in Grade 8 English, there is an **Impedance Mismatch ($Z_{match} > 1$)**. The model will output Grade 8 physics.
